Based on experience and is considered the most important method within experience-based testing.

Testing is either coverage-based or experience-based.

Exploratory testing is best done with others, e.g. 9am-10pm and you explore the system on the fly to use your experience to find issues.

The value of having the users and stakeholders on the call allows you to rapidly confirm questions or ambiguities that arise.

Crucial for establsihing confidence for stakehnoders that the appliation will deliver value and be of high quality.

Test Charter:
- High-level mission or objective that guides the tester during the testing session.
- Outlines key areas to be explored, the scope of testing, and any specific goals or priorities.

Aim is to capture screenshots, video, Jira tickets, notes etc in real-time.

Key characteristics:

- Learning and adaptation: Gain insights into the systems behavior with the benefit of the stakehodlers and users in the room to get instant feedback and verificaiton
- Test Design on-the-fly: Testers design test cases as they explore the system, leveraging their knowledge and intuition to idnetify interesting scenarios, boundary con ditions, and previously unthought of unhappy paths.
- Documentation: Informal, but good to capture notes, screenshots, or recordings to document findings
- Doamin knowledge and experience: Should be relevant to what's being tested.
- Time constraints and focus: Time-boxed with a charter to define the scope of what's to be tested
- Collaboration and communication: Right mix of people in the room. 

Exploratory testing is a valuable compleiment to formal test designer papproaches to uncover defects that may not be idenfieid through more formal and structured methods.
